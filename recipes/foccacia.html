Foccacia Bread

A fairly long and exhaustive focaccia recipe that only turns out good sometimes. Time to cook: 2 days.


Ingredients

  • 1/4oz. Active Dry Yeast
  • 2tsp Honey
  • 5cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 1tbsp Morten's Kosher Salt
  • 6tbsp EVOO, plus some for hands

Steps

  1. Whisk yeast, honey, and 2.5 cups of lukewarm water. Let sit for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 5 cups of flour and the salt to the bowl. Mix well until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Pour 4tbsp EVOO into a bowl for fridge. Transfer dough and turn to coat. Cover and chill until dough is doubled in size at least-- 8hrs-24hrs.
      You can also let the dough rise at room temp for 3-4hrs instead.
  4. Butter a 13x9" baking tray. Add 1tbsp EVOO into the center of the pan. Keeping the dough in the bowl, form dough into a ball, or deflate it. Transfer to buttered pan.
  5. Transfer the dough to the prepped buttered pan. Let rise, uncovered, in a dry, warm spot for 1.5-4hrs.
  6. Place oven rack in the middle. Preheat to 450 degrees.
  7. To see if the dough is ready, poke it with your finger. It should spring back slowly, leaving an indentation.
  8. Oil your hands lightly. Stretch dough to fit pan. Dimple foccacia aggressively. Drizzle with olive oil and salt.
  9. This is where you add your toppings. I personally steal rosemary from my neighbors garden, and then leave freshly baked bread on their doorstep in exchange.
    I have added kimchi in the past, which was very good, but required no theivery and therefore very little interest for me.
  10. Bake until golden brown all over-- 20-30mins.
